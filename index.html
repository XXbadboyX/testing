The HortiMed project

Entitled “Towards circular horticulture: Closing the loop on Mediterranean greenhouses” (Grant Number 1915) is an action implemented by INKOA SISTEMAS S.L. (INKOA, Spain, Coordinator), the National Institute of Oceanography and Fisheries (NIOF, Egypt, partner), the University Mohamed Khider, Biskra (UMKB, Algeria, partner) and Universidad de Deusto (UDeusto, Spain, partner).

The action was awarded a grant under the PRIMA Programme, which is supported by the European Union under Horizon 2020 the European Union’s Framework Programme for Research and Innovation.

The HortiMED project, which has an overall duration of 48 months, aims to provide the Mediterranean horticultural community with innovative tools to enable resource efficient year round greenhouse cultivation by harnessing the potential of both simple and advanced technologies for smart nutrient, irrigation & climate control, and integrated pest management taking into account their feasibility and cost-effectiveness at individual greenhouse level.

Objectives

The overall objective of the project is to provide the Mediterranean horticultural community with innovative tools to enable resource efficient year round greenhouse cultivation by harnessing the potential of both simple and advanced technologies for smart nutrient, irrigation & climate control, and integrated pest management taking into account their feasibility and cost-effectiveness at individual greenhouse level.

To achieve this overall objective the project is targeted at the following Specific Objectives (SO):

Smart Decision Support System

This system helps farmers with expert advice and automation for better greenhouse control.

IMTA-Aquaponics System

Demonstrate closed-loop aquaponics for sustainable Mediterranean produce.

Integrated Pest Management

Provide farmers with eco-friendly pest management tools.

Tech Validation

Validate HortiMED technologies and analyze their impact across different greenhouses.

Communicate Results

Effectively communicate project findings to embed them locally.

Work Plan

In order to achieve the objectives of the HortiMED project, a Work Package (WP) structure has been made to ensure the most effective performance of work and complementary ways to reach the planned objectives. The structure is also expected to easily monitor the work undertaken by the project partners. Five (5) WP have been defined covering different milestones and deliverables, organized as:

WP1 (System definition)

will be focused on the definition of the system based on the user, functional, technical and environmental requirements and specifications

WP2 (Definition of the key technical solutions)

This project focuses on resolving technical challenges, innovating, and defining HW/SW integration by designing the system architecture and interoperation mechanism.

WP3 (Prototype development and system validation)

will take the technology outputs from WP2 with the requirements and specifications defined in WP1 and validate the system in real operating conditions

WP4 (Effective transfer of HortiMED technologies)

This will focus on effectively transferring HortiMED technologies and disseminating project results. An updated Business Plan will also be developed.

WP5 (Project coordination and management)

will be focused on project management activities. WP5 is a transversal WP connected to all WPs and defines the management structure of the project and the communication with the PRIMA Secretariat/EC

Demo Sites

HortiMED will implement its innovative solutions in various demo sites across the Mediterranean region, showcasing the effectiveness of advanced water management, aquifer recharge, and water reuse practices in real-world settings.
